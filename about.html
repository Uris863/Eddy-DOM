Life

De Pretto grew up in the Parisian banlieue of Créteil. In his youth he played theater and later came to music. He eventually moved to Paris as an adult. He worked as an actor for TV commercials and he was involved in the short film Vivre sa vie in 2013. In 2014, he started working as a singer on a tour ship of the Compagnie des Bateaux Mouches. He worked there for three years. In 2017, he participated in the Musivfestival Printemps de Bourges, where he won the newcomer prize Inouïs. In October 2017, he released his debut EP Kid with four tracks. In January 2021, he released a single for the first time in a long time, Bateaux-Mouches. His second album, A tous les bâtards, followed in March 2021. In January 2021, he released a single for the first time in a long time, Bateaux-Mouches. Style

The songs on his debut EP Kid have been described as a blend of traditional chanson and hip-hop. In his live performances, he was accompanied only by a drummer. The songs on the EP deal with themes such as the pressure to appear masculine, homophobic violence, and the difficulty of finding one's identity. His music has also been compared to Stromae on several occasions. De Pretto himself further cites musicians such as Frank Ocean, Claude Nougaro, and Booba as inspiration.
